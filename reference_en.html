Effectiveness vs. Efficiency

Effectiveness is about achieving the intended outcome — it's focused on what you do.
In contrast, efficiency is about doing things with minimal waste — it’s about how you do it.

For example, writing clean meeting notes or automating repetitive tasks speaks to efficiency.
But if the meeting lacks strategy, or the notes don’t lead to action — it’s not effective.

It’s common to hear, “We’re working so hard, but why aren’t we seeing results?”
This often signals a team stuck in high efficiency, but low effectiveness.

Ultimately, the key is finding the right balance between the two:
setting the right direction first — then moving toward it without unnecessary waste.
